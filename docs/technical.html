1 · System overview

The system converts a raw UFC broadcast video into an annotated video in which every 5-second window carries: a fight / non-fight decision (replays, walkouts and breaks are detected and skipped), a phase label (Striking · Grappling/Ground Work · Clinch · Transition/Takedown · Neutral/Measuring Distance), a pressure label (Fighter 1 · Fighter 2 · Mutual — who is pushing the action), and fighter bounding boxes with persistent identities.

Fighter 1 convention — used consistently in labels, metadata and the UI: the fighter whose name appears left of the timer in the broadcast bottom overlay. Fighter 2 is the name on the right.

Three learned models cooperate, plus one non-learned identity module:

2 · Dataset & labeling protocol

The dataset is self-built with the Streamlit tool in tools/labeler.py: 11 full UFC fights were cut into consecutive, non-overlapping 5-second clips (1280×720 @ 29.97 fps, ≈150 frames each). For every clip the annotator either assigned one phase + one pressure label, or pressed Skip/Exclude — the clip is then saved with an _excluded filename suffix and no labels. Excluded clips (replays, walkouts, round breaks…) are kept on purpose: they are the positive class for the gate model.

Totals: 1,315 clips = 1,159 labeled fight clips + 156 excluded clips. Phase distribution: Striking 56% · Neutral 18% · Ground 16% · Transition 5% · Clinch 5%. Pressure: Mutual 49% · Fighter 2 27% · Fighter 1 24%. Imbalance is handled by loss weighting (§7), not by resampling.

Two shorts colors per fight (f1_color, f2_color) anchor identity at training time. Valid values map to explicit HSV ranges: red · blue · black · white · green · gold · gray · orange · purple. A fight without colors trains with all-zero masks (phase unaffected; pressure degrades — §4).

3 · Preprocessing & the clip cache

scripts/preprocess.py runs once and converts every clip (kept and excluded) into a compact .npz. Training never decodes video afterwards — the single biggest speed factor on free Colab. The script is resumable (existing files skipped).

  1. Decode 16 frames at indices round(linspace(0, n−1, 16)) (~1 frame / 0.33 s), full 1280×720 resolution.
  2. Detect fighters — YOLOv8-nano, person class, conf ≥ 0.35, top-2 by box area. Detection happens before any downscaling, so distant fighters are found at full resolution.
  3. Assign F1/F2 per frame by shorts color (§4).
  4. Propagate identity to ambiguous frames via IoU with neighboring frames (§4).
  5. Rasterize the mask at 128×228: +1 in F1's box, −1 in F2's box, 0 background; box-overlap regions forced to 0 (ownership unknown).
  6. Save frames (uint8, RGB, 16×128×228×3) + mask (int8) ≈ 1 MB/clip, ≈ 1.5 GB total.
The script ends with a per-fight identity coverage report — fraction of frames with both fighters assigned. A fight under 0.5 is flagged <-- CHECK COLORS: usually a wrong or ambiguous color in the meta CSV.

4 · The identity module

Why it must exist. Pressure labels name a person, but “Fighter 1” is broadcast metadata (name left of the timer) — invisible in the action pixels. Two pixel-identical clips can carry opposite pressure labels. Without identity input, the label is not a function of the input, and no model can beat the base rate. The mask channel makes “Fighter 1 pressuring” the learnable pattern “the +1 region is advancing”.

Decision rules

  • Shorts region of a box: rows 35–60% of height, cols 15–85% of width (trunk/shorts, avoids gloves/canvas). Converted to HSV; each named color has fixed ranges (e.g. red = H∈[0,12]∪[168,180]; black = V≤70,S≤100); highest pixel-ratio color wins.
  • Per-frame assignment: exact two-sided match → both assigned; one-sided match with no contradiction → the other detection becomes the other fighter; both ambiguous → frame unassigned.
  • Temporal propagation: forward then backward sweep; an unassigned frame borrows identity from its nearest assigned neighbor by matching boxes with IoU > 0.2 (fighters barely move in 0.33 s). Still-unassigned frames yield zero mask.
The mask is best-effort, not ground truth — referees can steal a top-2 slot, similar shorts confuse colors, clinches merge boxes. The classifier trains with these imperfect masks; the --no-mask ablation quantifies the channel's value.

Parameters are drawn once per clip and applied to all 16 frames and the mask → temporal coherence and RGB↔mask alignment are preserved. The flip is label-safe for pressure because identity travels in the mask, not in screen position. Validation/inference use a plain resize of the full frame to 112×112 (content shrunk, never cropped away). The gate dataset uses the same machinery with single frames: 4 frames per clip (slots 0/5/10/15), label = the clip's excluded flag.

6 · Model architectures

Channel inflation (shared trick)

Pretrained first convs expect 3 channels; clips have 4. A new first conv is built with identical geometry, pretrained RGB weights copied into channels 0–2, and the mask channel zero-initialized — at init the network is bit-for-bit the pretrained RGB model; mask weights grow only where useful. Training is standard fine-tuning: nothing frozen, backbone at 10× lower LR than new modules.

7 · Training procedure & cross-validation

  • Loss: L = CEphase + 0.5·CEpressure, both with label smoothing ε = 0.05 and per-class weights wc = N/(K·nc) computed on the training fold (a Clinch error ≈ 11× a Striking error).
  • Optimizer: AdamW, wd 1e-4; two groups — backbone 3e-5, new modules 3e-4. Mixed precision on CUDA.
  • Schedule & stopping: ReduceLROnPlateau (×0.5, patience 2) and early stopping (patience 6, max 25 epochs) on validation phase macro-F1 (pressure macro-F1 for --task pressure); best checkpoint reloaded before eval.
  • Folds: StratifiedGroupKFold(4) grouped by fight — a validation fight never contributes training clips (no leakage); stratified on phase. --lofo = leave-one-fight-out (11 folds). Every fold saves its checkpoint, predictions and history → a dead Colab session resumes with --folds i,j.
  • Reported numbers: out-of-fold concatenated predictions → macro-F1, per-class report, confusion matrices, and a per-fight table for failure analysis. Known impurity (stated in the report): early stopping tunes the epoch on the reported fold; nested CV would fix it at 3× compute.
  • Deployment model: --final retrains on all 11 fights, fixed 12 epochs, no validation — used only by inference, never for reported metrics.
  • Gate: fight-grouped 75/25 split, BCE with pos_weight ≈ 7.6, 8 epochs; best-val-AUC checkpoint; operating threshold = max-F1 point of the validation PR curve, stored inside gate.pt.

8 · Inference pipeline

python scripts/infer.py --video fight.mp4 — single pass over the video, windows buffered in memory and written straight to the output writer (no physical split/concat).

Per-window details

  1. Gate — 4 of the 16 sampled frames → resize 112² → mean sigmoid = P(non-fight); above the stored threshold the window is stamped and steps 2–5 are skipped.
  2. Detect & track — YOLO boxes per frame carry no identity; the greedy IoU linker joins a detection to the track whose last box overlaps most (IoU > 0.3), else opens a new track. The two tracks with the largest cumulative area are the fighters (referee boxes are smaller/intermittent).
  3. Identity — one-time bootstrap (terminal prompt with a saved A/B image, or declared shorts colors), then per-window matching of each track's HSV appearance histogram (H×S, 30×32 bins over torso+shorts, averaged along the track) against two EMA-updated anchors. Appearance survives camera cuts, so identity does too.
  4. Classify — masks rasterized exactly as in training; frames resized to 112², normalized with the deployed model's stats; softmax gives labels + confidences.
  5. Overlay — box coordinates linearly interpolated between sampled frames (box(t) = (1−α)·box(ti) + α·box(ti+1)) so they move smoothly across all ~150 frames; banner dims when phase confidence < 0.5.

After the last window the original audio is remuxed onto the annotated video (best-effort ffmpeg), and a JSON log is written with one record per window: {window, start_s, gate_prob_excluded, excluded, phase, pressure, phase_conf, pressure_conf}.
